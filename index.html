What Does a Podiatrist Do?

A podiatrist is a healthcare professional who focuses on the structure and function of the feet and lower limbs. They have expertise in:

  • Diagnosing sports injuries
  • Providing non-surgical and surgical treatments
  • Prescribing orthotics
  • Advising on prevention and rehabilitation

Common Sports Injuries Treated by Podiatrists

1. Ankle Sprains

Ankle sprains occur when the ligaments in your ankle stretch or tear due to sudden twisting or rolling. A podiatrist can assess the severity of the injury, recommend physical therapy, and suggest bracing or taping to support the joint during recovery.

2. Plantar Fasciitis

Plantar fasciitis is a common condition causing heel pain, often due to overuse or improper footwear. A podiatrist can provide relief through custom orthotics, stretching exercises, and advanced treatments like shockwave therapy.

3. Stress Fractures

Stress fractures are small cracks in the bone caused by repetitive stress or overtraining. A podiatrist will guide you in reducing activity levels, wearing protective footwear, and monitoring healing progress.

4. Achilles Tendonitis

Inflammation of the Achilles tendon often results from excessive running or jumping. Podiatrists offer therapies such as stretching, icing, and footwear modifications to alleviate pain and prevent further damage.

5. Shin Splints

Pain along the shinbone is a common issue for runners. A podiatrist can help identify contributing factors like overpronation and provide orthotics or strengthening exercises to reduce discomfort.

What to Expect During a Visit to a Podiatrist

Your initial consultation with a podiatrist will typically include:

  • Detailed History: The podiatrist will ask about your injury, physical activity, and any previous issues.
  • Physical Exam: Assessment of your feet, ankles, and lower legs for swelling, tenderness, or misalignment.
  • Diagnostic Tests: X-rays or other imaging tests may be ordered if needed.
  • Treatment Plan: Recommendations for therapy, orthotics, or further interventions based on the findings.

Tips to Avoid Sports Injuries
